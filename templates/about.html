Overview of Arecanut Diseases

Arecanut disease, also known as Koleroga, Mahali, fruit rot, or bud rot, is a fungus-caused disease that causes arecanut fruits to rot. The fungus is Phytophthora areeae and this disease is common in areas that receive heavy rainfall. The initial symptoms include yellowish or dark green water-soaked lesions on the nut's surface. The lesions spread and cover the entire fruit, which then rots. The main symptom of the disease is yellowing of leaves. The yellowing starts from the tips of the leaflets of the outer leaves gradually extending along the margin to the middle of the lamina. The affected leaves often develop necrosis from their tips. In advanced stages, the leaves become smaller, stiff and pointed.

Symptoms of Arecanut disease

  • 1. Leaf Spotting: Circular or irregular spots on the leaves, often with a yellow or brownish center.

  • 2. Leaf Yellowing: Yellowing of the leaves, which may indicate nutrient deficiencies or disease.

  • 3. Leaf Blight: Rapid browning and wilting of leaves, often leading to leaf death.

  • 4. Nut Rot: Rotting of the nuts, often starting at the tip and progressing towards the base.

  • 5. Crown Rot: Rotting of the crown (base) of the plant, leading to wilting and death of the plant.

  • 6. Stem Canker: Dark, sunken lesions on the stem, which can girdle the plant and cause wilting.

  • 7. Root Rot: Rotting of the roots, leading to reduced nutrient uptake and wilting of the plant.

  • 8. Fruit Rot: Rotting of the fruits, often starting at the blossom end and spreading throughout the fruit.

  • 9. Twig Dieback: Death of the tips of branches or twigs, often accompanied by darkening and drying of the affected areas.

  • 10. Reduced Growth: Overall stunted growth of the plant, which can be caused by various diseases or nutrient deficiencies.
